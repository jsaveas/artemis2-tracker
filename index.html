📍 Current Phase
Flight Day 3 — Outbound Coast to the Moon

TLI burn completed April 2 at 7:49 PM EDT. The 5-minute 50-second main engine firing accelerated Orion to break free of Earth orbit, placing the crew on a free-return trajectory around the lunar far side. Orion is now in cislunar space, coasting toward the Moon. Crew is conducting health checks, CPR practice in microgravity, medical equipment demos, and practicing lunar photography ahead of the April 6 flyby. A minor toilet fan issue and a cabin pressure warning (deemed non-critical) were noted but resolved.
⏭️ Next Milestone
🌙 Lunar Flyby — April 6

Orion will swing around the Moon's far side, photographing partially-illuminated terrain. The far side shadow geometry will reveal ridges, crater rims, and slopes. On Day 6, crew will also observe a solar eclipse — sun's corona visible from deep space.
